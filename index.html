AR Filters & Appearance Study

You are invited to participate in a psychology research study at Sunway University examining how augmented reality (AR) social media filters influence state appearance self-esteem and appearance satisfaction among young adults in Malaysia.

⏱ ~20–25 min 🔁 2 Trials 📱 Researcher device provided 🔒 Fully anonymous
What will you do? You will be randomly assigned to one of two conditions: a beautifying AR filter or a non-beautifying AR filter. You will complete two trials — Trial 1 using your assigned condition, and Trial 2 without any filter. Questionnaires are completed after each trial.
Selfie verification: You will be asked to upload your selfie photograph after each trial to confirm the task was completed. Your photograph will be retained securely as part of the research record, with your informed consent.
Risks: Minimal. You may experience mild, temporary appearance-related self-consciousness. A full debriefing will be provided after the study. You may withdraw at any time without penalty.

Purpose of This Study

This research examined whether exposure to beautifying AR filters, non-beautifying AR filters, or no filter produces measurable differences in state appearance self-esteem and appearance satisfaction among young adults in Malaysia.

The Appearance Self-Esteem subscale of the State Self-Esteem Scale (Heatherton & Polivy, 1991) and the Appearance Evaluation subscale of the MBSRQ (Cash, 2000) were used to capture your immediate responses across both trials.

AR filters apply digital enhancements that do not represent realistic appearance standards. Any temporary feelings of appearance-related discomfort following this study are expected to resolve shortly.
Your selfie photographs are stored securely on a password-encrypted external hard drive and will be permanently deleted upon completion of the research, in accordance with the university's data retention policy and your informed consent.
